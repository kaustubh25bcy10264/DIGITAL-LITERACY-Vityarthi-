Social Media Do’s and Don’ts for College Students

✅ Do’s
Think before posting: Share content that reflects positively on your character and professionalism.

Respect privacy: Adjust privacy settings and avoid oversharing personal information.

Credit sources: Give proper attribution when sharing academic or creative work.

Maintain professionalism: Use appropriate language and tone, especially on platforms visible to faculty or recruiters.

Engage responsibly: Participate in discussions constructively and avoid spreading misinformation.

❌ Don’ts
Don’t post impulsively: Avoid sharing content when angry, upset, or without reflection.

Don’t plagiarize: Copying others’ work without credit is unethical and can harm your reputation.

Don’t overshare: Avoid posting sensitive details like addresses, phone numbers, or academic records.

Don’t blur boundaries: Keep personal jokes or memes separate from professional/academic platforms.

Don’t engage in cyberbullying: Never use social media to insult, harass, or demean others.
